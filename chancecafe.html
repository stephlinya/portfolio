Overview.

Problem

Cat cafes are typically popular in East Asia; however, majority of these cats are not up for adoption. Small businesses in the United States have tried to transform the typical cat cafe to be a meeting ground for interested adopters and homeless cats.

As a cat lover, I noticed many of these sites tend to be a little disorganized and difficult to navigate around. I created Chance Cafe as an opportunity to practicing my branding, design, and technical skills.

Solution

A responsive website that shares the love of cats with clear information hierarchy and cohesive branding.

Role

I completed all branding, design, writing, and code on my own.

Project status

This project is in progress. The dynamic catalog on the "cats" page features the REST PetFinder API, which currently only displays on my local environment.

Competitive analysis.

Crumbs and Whiskers.

pros

  • strong brand identity
  • lots of images - really shows love of cats!

cons

  • difficult to read some of the headings
  • difficult to read reservation page, very text heavy
  • some confusing nav items, even more options on dropdown

The Whole Cat.

pros

  • compassionate mission statement
  • clear sections

cons

  • cluttered navigation and hero image
  • lack of CTAs
  • body font size is quite small
  • width of text is long, making it harder to read
  • a lot of different font sizes across different pages in the site

Neko Cat Cafe.

pros

  • strong landing page with lots of CTAs, cohesive brand, lots of images
  • features adoptable cats

cons

  • hero image has too many CTAs - should link to the general reservation page
  • some parts of the website isn't responsive, with texts bleeding over
  • lots of all uppercase text - feels too high energy
  • featured cards for non-adoptable cats
  • nav is hard to read unless on sticky scroll
